FADE IN:

EXT. MALL PARKING LOT - DAY

A beige 1982 AMC Eagle sits in the sun. The rear hatch stands open.

Two men heave a 30-pound Compaq portable computer toward the car. They lug it rather than carry it. They drop it into the back of the Eagle.

INT. SUNNYVALE TOWN CENTER MALL - DAY

1970s architecture. Brown and orange tile floors. Foliage in concrete planters.

Middle-class shoppers pass by. An elderly man with a cane. A mother pushing a stroller. A woman in designer heels.

INT. RADIOSHACK - CONTINUOUS

Consumer electronics line the shelves. CB radios. Remote control cars. A Tandy CoCo 2 sits on a shelf with a $160 price tag.

A folding table holds the Compaq computer running demo software. A sign reads "FREE DEMO."

Orion (30s, thoughtful presence) stands beside the table. The Narrator watches from nearby.

A MIDDLE-AGED WOMAN approaches the keyboard. She presses keys. Nothing happens. She presses harder. She drags her fingers across all the keys at once.

The screen remains static.

Her face tightens. Her shoulders tense.

ORION
(quietly)
When I see a user get frustrated with the software, I think this is the hardest time to listen. And also, the time we must listen the hardest.

The woman jabs at the keys.

ORION (CONT'D)
Look. The user isn't misbehaving. Her attitude is mirroring the machine.

He approaches her. His posture softens. He makes eye contact.

ORION (CONT'D)
Our software is the one that is failing you.

The woman's shoulders drop. Her face relaxes.

A CHILD turns the mouse upside down and tries to roll it like a trackball.

Orion surveys the store. He drags his hand along the side of a Tandy CoCo 2.

ORION
I hear the words "personal computer" and I think about machines that respect each of us as unrepeatable creations.

He looks at the Narrator.

ORION (CONT'D)
With these machines, do we commodify the human or accommodate the person?

He watches the child with the upside-down mouse.

ORION (CONT'D)
Whether we do or not... this all depends on the choices we make, every day. I need to make the right ones.

CLOSE ON: Orion's hand on the computer. The woman's hand on the keyboard. The child's hand on the mouse.

The frame softens. Colors blur and connect like edges on a graph. Compassion made visible. Vectors of warmth between human and machine.

The moment crystallizes—

Then dissolves.

EXT. MALL PARKING LOT - LATE AFTERNOON

The Compaq goes back into the Eagle. 4:00 PM light stretches across the asphalt.

INT. AMC EAGLE - MOVING - DAY

The car pulls onto the road. The Narrator drives. Orion rides passenger.

NARRATOR
So what did you think?

ORION
It's broken.

NARRATOR
What part?

ORION
The premise.

The car passes storefronts.

ORION (CONT'D)
It's not any particular piece. It's the promise we're communicating to the user. We say one thing, the interface says another, and it behaves in yet another.

He watches the road.

ORION (CONT'D)
It's within these mixed messages that the opportunity is lost. Everything must be spoken with a single voice.

NARRATOR
And what does that voice say?

ORION
I dunno.

A beat.

ORION (CONT'D)
Hey, look, I care about you. Your stress, anxiety, and suffering—it means something to me. I'm here for you. To support you.

NARRATOR
You mean like a therapist?

ORION
No, not exactly.

He gestures with his hands.

ORION (CONT'D)
Generous undo. Multiple ways to achieve something. Search and suggest instead of pecking through hierarchies.

The car continues down the road.

ORION (CONT'D)
People use software to get things done. We need to help them get there. Not present intellectual exercises.

FADE OUT.
